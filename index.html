My Work

  • Self-made Linux distribution

    Got challenged to develop a linux distro all by myself, accepted it and crushed it. Named it BinodOS becauase back in the days, that meme was really funny. Check out the demo video. Also, I edited and synced the video myself.

    Features and Highlights:
    • Very light on the system. Uses around 135 MB of ram space while idle (with background processes and daemons running). Uses less than 2 GB even with a video editor open.
    • Global dark mode. Because it looks good to me.
    • Global Ad Blocking with personalized port blocking. Hate it when companies, for some reason, invest more in audience research and monitoring with cookies and ads than they invest in their damn product development. Block every single port you don't need and sleep in peace knowing practically no one other than you can let those viruses and malewares in.
    • Global search with FZF enabled to help you grab things quickly. When you open them, Vim's intelligent file-type AI figures out the type and opens them with a software with the least load at that time. And for every file you decide to edit, the integrated code editor will download, install, setup and optimize the toolchain and environment for that type to help you work.
    • Due to the light resource usage, your kernel can readily produce spaace and processing power for your tasks, unlike with Windows.
    • Comes with a minimalistic I3-wmdesktop environment for keyboard driven workflow. It also helps with carpal tunnel that most developers are statistically proven to develop due to using a mouse.
    • Remains free. My 16 year old self that made it didn't realize the importance of money to give it away. And I'm too noble to put a pay wall. So yes, it is still free. Sadly I did not generate revenue to keep the servers going, so you'll have to ask me personally (nicely) for a copy of the OS.
